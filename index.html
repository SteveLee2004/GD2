    WHAT IS KINO?

    KINO is an archive of film formats that have emerged throughout over a century of cinema. It brings together the historical context and characteristics of each format, along with the distinct images produced by films shot in them. These platforms are not merely specifications of cameras or the size of a theater screen. Each format generates a different kind of image through its own texture, resolution, and frame. This goes beyond technical difference—it reshapes the very way we perceive cinema. Texture, grain, resolution, frame size. These are not simply technical elements, but a language that constructs the image of film. Cinema is not only defined by what it shows, but by how it is captured. KINO documents film not in terms of what is shown, but how it is shown. 16mm, 35mm, 70mm, VistaVision, IMAX. Each format carries its own history and way of seeing.
